This project really couldn't have hit me at a better time, and I'm really ashamed to say I found it because of a slop Instagram reel that disgraced (or maybe, in this case, graced) my feed.

A boring white dude listing his "favorite albums without lyrics". The top 3 were *Kind of Blue*, *Giant Steps*, and *The Black Saint and the Sinner Lady*, confirming my suspicion that these three (plus *A Love Supreme*) are the only jazz albums annoying music nerds have ever listened to. Buried in between the Barnes and Noble jazz vinyl section classics was *Two Airships / Exploder Falls*. Something about the rawness of the cover, the two stylish little sweeps that look like the tail on a sixteenth note, drew me in immediately. I'm a sucker for interesting album covers.

I embark on my final night drive to Berkeley before the semester starts. I hit play. The car speakers suck me in. **It's a miracle I ever got to Berkeley that night.**

Someone invented the emotion capture technology from Monsters Inc., hired a gang of monsters to make people happy, and distilled pure liquid euphoria into an MP3. *Two Airships* kicks it off with the most accurate sonic depiction of manic happiness I've ever heard. The mix is incredibly noisy yet intelligible, the guitars and synths cutting through the fuzz to build a 12 minute long truimphant anthem. The guitar riffs are simple, but their simplicity remains one of their biggest strengths as they decorate the mighty, harmonic wall of fuzz that drives the track forward. This track had me dancing in the drivers seat so intently I was swerving a bit. I headbanged from Brentwood to Walnut Creek, and as I sensed the track was coming to a close, I was already seated for more.

Is this project too good to be true? Is *Exploder Falls* gonna ruin all of this momentum?

Hahahaha! No!!!!!!!! It's even fucking better!!!!!!!!!!!!

*Exploder Falls* feels less manic and a little more introspective than *Two Airships*, but incredibly joyful nonetheless. It tends to dwell on ideas for just a bit longer on average than *Two Airships*, but I feel that it absolutely makes up for it in the pure intensity of the many highs across the track's 17-minute runtime. I particularly **love** the part at about 5 minutes in where a mid-tone bass groove hops into the fray to carry the track forward, only to open up to one of the most satisfying walls of fuzz and synth melodies I've ever heard. The bass groove then steps back and integrates itself into the track, finding its place among the vast palette of themes this project can play on.

As I write this note, I'm having a really great couple of weeks. I'm so excited to get out there and meet new people and have the best fucking semester of my educational journey thus far. This project more or less perfectly captures the raw, unfiltered optimism I'm feeling.
